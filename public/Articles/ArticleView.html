Dreams

What are Dreams ?

Dreams are stories and images that our minds create while we sleep. They can be entertaining, fun, romantic, disturbing, frightening, and sometimes bizarre. They are an enduring source of mystery for scientists and psychological doctors. Why do dreams occur? What causes them? Can we control them? What do they mean?

How do we dream ?

There are a number of phases that our brains go through in cycles throughout the night. In the first phase we Transition from wakefullness to sleep as we begin to relax and breathing slows . And now into Light Sleep as body temperature drops ad breathing slows down even more . After that comes Deep sleep characterized by a particular pattern in your brain called delta waves . Finally comes the REM sleep Breathing gets faster, and your eye moves all over. During this stage is when dreaming happens . Through this phase the brain is active almost as active as how it is during the day

Why are dreams significant ?

From a scientific paradigm, dreams evolved to ensure that the part of the brain that handles vision(i.e The visual cortex or Occipital lobe) does not deprecate while we sleep(basically keep it alive) This is usually to ensure that your brain can adequately proccess what you see. From an evolutionary perspective, dreams worked as a virtual environment to the cave man in order to simulate dangerous situations that might arise in the wild . From a spiritual perspective , dreams happen to prepare us of future events and has made its way to some gospel writings as an insight of whats to come. I wont give you a definitive answer as to why they happen

Activation Synthesis Theory

The activation-synthesis model suggests that dreams are caused by the physiological processes of the brain. While people used to believe that sleeping and dreaming was a passive process, researchers now know that the brain is anything but quiet during sleep.

Controling your dreams can be a daunting yet satisfying trial, here's why. While some say it would be trivial to do so, I think a good dream can be a prequel to an awesome day.
